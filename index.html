Written language is experienced as form rather than symbol. For some synesthetes, letters are not abstract signs but spatial entities with weight, direction, and geometry. Language becomes something shaped and seen before it is understood.

For some synesthetes, color is an inseparable part of language. Letters do not appear as neutral marks, but arrive with their own chromatic presence. A word is already tinted before it is read, and its color remains stable, familiar, and personal.

This website explores synesthesia as a way of understanding how the world is perceived when senses intertwine. Instead of relying on theory, the project translates synesthesia into interactive, sensory-based experiences. All texts and quotations throughout the site are drawn from real testimonies of syntesthetes, reflecting their personal and subjective experiences of letter, number, sound, color, shape, and emotion. Synesthesia is not a metaphor or an imaginative association, but a consistent and involuntary way of perceiving the world.
